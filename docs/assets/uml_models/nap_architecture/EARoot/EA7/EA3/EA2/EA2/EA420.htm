<b>“Support of commonly used languages”</b> KPI may take four possible values. A value equal to 0 indicates that a NAP supports only the national language. A value equal to 1 indicates that although a NAP supports English, some of its content (e.g., metadata of included publications) is provided only in the national language. On the other hand, a value equal to 2 indicates that a NAP fully supports the English language.<br/><br/><i>Note 1: This is relevant to the webpage and also to the metadata/data content.</i><br/><i>Note 2: “To support some language” means to have appropriate interface and content available in that language version</i><br/><i>Note 3: This KPI is about the output from the NAP: you also may consider the automatic translation of items depending on selected language, i.e., it is not necessary to have an item in a NAP saved in EN version if the NAP is able to export it in such a EN version if selected by the user</i><br/><br/>Values:<br/><b>0: </b>Supports national language only, other than EN<br/><b>1: </b>Supports EN for platform (user interface) only <br/><b>2: </b>Supports EN for platform (user interface) and names of the datasets  <br/><b>3: </b>Supports EN for platform (user interface) and all metadata content (all text content incl. data description)<br/><br/>Minimum acceptable value for interoperability: 2<br/><br/>
