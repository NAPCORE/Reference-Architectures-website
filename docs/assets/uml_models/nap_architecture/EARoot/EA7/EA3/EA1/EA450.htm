<b>Type</b>: Legal<br/><b>Full Name: </b>Regulation (EU) 2018/1724 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 2 October 2018 establishing a single digital gateway to provide access to information, to procedures and to assistance and problem-solving services and amending Regulation (EU) No 1024/2012<br/><b>Link:</b> <a href="file://https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2018/1724/oj"><font color="#467886"><u>https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2018/1724/oj</u></font></a><br/><b>Description</b>: Regulation (EU) 2018/1724, known as the <i>Single Digital Gateway (SDG) Regulation</i>, establishes a unified EU-wide portal—<b>Your Europe</b>—that offers cross-border users streamlined access to public services, procedures, and information. The regulation aims to improve the usability, accessibility, and multilingual availability of digital public services. It also introduces the <b>once-only principle</b>, meaning that public administrations should not ask citizens or businesses to resubmit information already provided to another authority.<br/>While not transport-specific, the regulation applies to National Access Points (NAPs) when they serve as public-facing digital platforms operated by authorities. If NAPs provide access to regulated datasets or procedures relevant to cross-border users, they must meet accessibility, quality, and multilingual standards. The once-only principle also supports federated data publishing, reducing duplication between NAPs and national open data portals.<br/><b>Additional details</b>: The SDG’s <b>once-only principle</b> supports a more streamlined and federated approach to data publishing. The goal is to <b>avoid redundant data publication and collection</b>—public sector data should be published <i>once</i> and reused or accessed via interoperable systems rather than duplicated. This has practical implications for NAPs and open data portals:<br/><ul>
<li>Transport datasets required under ITS delegated regulations must be made available via the NAP.</li><li>The same datasets may also be subject to publication obligations under the Open Data Directive, typically via a national open data portal.</li></ul>
To resolve this, Member States should implement a <b>federated data publishing model</b> where the NAP serves as the authoritative data provider and the open data portal <b>references or harvests</b> its metadata. This avoids duplication while ensuring discoverability, open licensing, and machine-readability under both legal regimes.<br/><b>Related Documents:</b><br/><ul>
<li><a href="file://https://europa.eu/youreurope/index_en.htm"><font color="#467886"><u>Your Europe Portal</u></font></a> – The operational interface of the Single Digital Gateway, which may link to or integrate with national portals like NAPs for transport-related procedures.</li><li><a href="file://https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2022/1463"><font color="#467886"><u>Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2020/1463</u></font></a> – Sets out detailed rules on the quality of information and procedures provided via the gateway, including accessibility, findability, and user-centric design—relevant for NAP interfaces.</li><li><a href="file://https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2022/1121"><font color="#467886"><u>Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2022/1121</u></font></a> – Defines the technical and operational requirements for the feedback mechanism under the gateway, which may apply to NAPs if integrated into the SDG ecosystem.</li></ul><p/>
