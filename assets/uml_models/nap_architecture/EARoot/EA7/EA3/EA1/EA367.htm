<b>Type</b>: Legal<br/><b>Full Name: </b>Regulation (EU) 2023/1804 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 13 September 2023 on the deployment of alternative fuels infrastructure, and repealing Directive 2014/94/EU<br/><b>Link:</b> <a href="file://https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2023/1804/oj"><font color="#467886"><u>https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2023/1804/oj</u></font></a> <br/><b>Description</b>: The new <b>Alternative Fuels Infrastructure Regulation (AFIR)</b>, which repeals Directive 2014/94/EU. It establishes binding targets for the deployment of <b>publicly accessible recharging and refuelling infrastructure</b> for electric and hydrogen vehicles, vessels, and aircraft across the EU. It aims to ensure full <b>interoperability</b>, <b>user-friendliness</b>, and <b>data transparency</b> to support the EU’s climate and mobility goals.<br/><b>Additional details</b>: While AFIR does not explicitly mandate publication via NAPs, it requires <b>real-time and static data</b> on alternative fuels infrastructure (e.g. location, availability, pricing, technical characteristics) to be made <b>publicly accessible</b> in <b>machine-readable formats</b>. This aligns with the role of NAPs under the ITS Directive and its delegated acts, making them a <b>natural access point</b> for such datasets—especially where they support routing, parking, or multimodal travel services.<br/><b>Related Documents:</b><br/><ul>
