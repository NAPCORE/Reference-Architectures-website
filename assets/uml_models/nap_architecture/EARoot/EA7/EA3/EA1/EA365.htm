<b>Type</b>: Legal<br/><b>Full Name:</b> Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2022/670 of 2 February 2022 supplementing Directive 2010/40/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council with regard to the provision of EU-wide real-time traffic information services <br/><b>Link:</b> <a href="file://https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_del/2022/670/oj"><font color="#467886"><u>https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_del/2022/670/oj</u></font></a> <br/><b>Description:</b> This regulation defines binding technical specifications for the provision of EU-wide real-time traffic information (RTTI) services. It repeals Delegated Regulation (EU) 2015/962 and introduces updated requirements for the availability, accessibility, and reusability of static and dynamic road traffic data. Compared to the repealed act, it clarifies data categories, strengthens obligations for dynamic data updates, and adjusts performance requirements to reflect the current state of ITS deployment and operational data exchange practices. It enforces harmonised data formats and minimum service levels to ensure interoperability and continuity of RTTI services across Member States.<br/><b>Additional details:</b> National Access Points (NAPs) are the mandatory interface for publishing RTTI-relevant datasets. Under this regulation, NAPs must:<br/><ul>
<li>Provide <b>machine-readable access</b> to static and dynamic data elements listed in Annex I and II,</li><li>Ensure <b>data quality control</b> and <b>update frequency</b> in line with Annex III,</li><li>Use <b>DATEX II</b> or equivalent formats to guarantee semantic and syntactic interoperability,</li><li>Support <b>cross-border data exchange</b> by aligning with EU-wide service coverage and latency thresholds,</li><li>Maintain <b>metadata and service documentation</b> to enable automated discovery and integration by third parties.</li></ul>
